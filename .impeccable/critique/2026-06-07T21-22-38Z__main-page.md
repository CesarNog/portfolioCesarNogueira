## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated?**

**LLM assessment:** At first glance, no — and that's the surface-level win. Dark near-black surface, person-first hero (face before metrics), editorial section layouts, no gradient text, no hero-metric SaaS template as the primary beat. The "AI slop test" first-order check passes.

Second-order: two tells. (1) The font stack (Inter Tight + Geist + Geist Mono) is the Vercel/Next.js portfolio fingerprint of 2025-2026 — fully documented in the `main-page-fonts` critique, severity P2. (2) Section eyebrows (`font-mono text-xs uppercase tracking-[0.2em]`) appear on 5 of 8 sections — exactly the "eyebrow on every section = AI grammar" pattern the design rules flag. The eyebrow shows up on Experience, Work, Trust, Capabilities, Certifications. Contact and Testimonials use `noEyebrow`. The awareness is there; the application is inconsistent.

**Deterministic scan:** 1 finding — `em-dash-overuse` in `app/layout.tsx` (8 em-dashes in body text, including the meta title template at line 23). No gradient-text, no side-stripe borders, no repeating-gradient stripes, no sketchy SVG, no 32px+ border-radius on cards detected.

**Visual overlays:** Browser visualization not run this session (assessment run sequentially, no injection).

## Overall Impression

The page earns its premium positioning. Person-first, dark, restrained, no SaaS noise. The standout quality is the power-user trifecta (⌘K command palette, RecruiterMode, chatbot) — genuinely differentiated. The biggest opportunity is the eyebrow pattern: removing eyebrows from section headers would eliminate the most recognizable AI grammar tell in the layout, and the sections don't need them — the display h2s are strong enough.

---

## What's Working

**1. Person-first hero with typewriter + spotlight.** Face and name land before any metrics or credibility widgets. The typewriter + blur-in entrance is unhurried and purposeful — the opposite of "exploding gradient" hero AI defaults. On desktop, the portrait-as-right-column solves the photo placement elegantly.

**2. Power-user trifecta (H7 = 4/4).** Command palette (⌘K), RecruiterMode scanner, and the Hiring Assistant chatbot coexist without competing. Recruiters who know what they want get there fast; first-timers never see the complexity. Exceptional for this category.

**3. Editorial section layouts.** Capabilities (horizontal rows, no card borders), Testimonials (alternating-indent pull quotes), Experience (accordion timeline with scroll-driven beam) — each section gets its own visual vocabulary instead of repeating the card-grid reflex. This is disciplined.

## Priority Issues

### [P2] Section eyebrows on 5 of 8 sections

**What:** `Section` wrapper renders `font-mono text-xs uppercase tracking-[0.2em] text-[var(--color-fg-subtle)]` above every h2 by default. Used on Experience, Work, Trust, Capabilities, Certifications. Contact and Testimonials opt out via `noEyebrow`.

**Why it matters:** The "tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section" is explicitly on the absolute bans list — it appears in 55-95% of AI-generated layouts regardless of brief. The inconsistent use (5 of 8) is worse than either all-or-nothing: it reads as random, not as a deliberate system.

**Fix:** Remove `noEyebrow` from the `Section` API entirely. Let the display h2 headings stand on their own — they have enough scale and weight. If you want a section marker, use a different cadence: a horizontal rule, a colored dot, a number (only where sequence carries meaning), or nothing. The eyebrow adds no information not already in the heading.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable polish` (targeted: `components/sections/section.tsx` and all Section consumers)

---

### [P2] Em-dash in meta title template

**What:** `app/layout.tsx:23` — `template: \`%s — ${siteConfig.name}\`` — em-dash surfaces in every inner-page `<title>` tag and Twitter card metadata.

**Why it matters:** Brand rule is explicit: no em dashes. This one appears in SEO and social share surfaces that exist outside the visual design.

**Fix:**
```diff
- template: `%s — ${siteConfig.name}`,
+ template: `%s · ${siteConfig.name}`,
```

**Suggested command:** Direct edit (5 minutes, no command needed)

---

### [P2] Certifications cards: ghost-card + double accent

**What:** `components/sections/certifications.tsx:116` — cards have `border` (full colored border via `borderColor: accent.border`) + `hover:shadow-lg` + a separate `h-0.5` top accent stripe inside. Three decoration layers competing.

**Why it matters:** `border + shadow` on hover is the ghost-card pattern (even hover-only). The inner top stripe duplicates the signal the border already carries. Three visual accent channels on one card is noise, not emphasis.

**Fix:** Pick one decoration system per card. Either: (a) solid `panel` class + no inner stripe, rely on border color alone, or (b) remove the colored `borderColor` and keep only the inner top stripe. Drop `hover:shadow-lg` — use `hover:border-[var(--color-hairline-strong)]` instead (like the Projects cards do).

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable polish` (targeted: `components/sections/certifications.tsx`)

---

### [P3] "Stories" nav label for case-studies section

**What:** `components/site-header.tsx:12` — `{ href: "work", label: "Stories" }`. The section is case studies / project outcomes; "Stories" is abstract and doesn't map to a recruiter's mental model of "work samples."

**Why it matters:** Primary users (tech recruiters) scan for evidence. "Stories" may not trigger recognition. Even "Work" or "Projects" is clearer.

**Fix:** Consider "Work" or "Cases." Keep "Stories" only if the brand register deliberately favors the editorial framing — but note that the nav is a wayfinding tool, not a brand expression surface.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify` (nav labels)

---

### [P3] Contact blockquote: ring-1 vs border inconsistency

**What:** `components/sections/contact-console.tsx:38` — uses `ring-1 ring-[var(--color-blue)]/15` while DESIGN.md defines the inline callout pattern as `border border-[var(--color-blue)]/20`. Different CSS property, different opacity value.

**Why it matters:** Inconsistency with the design system token pattern. Minor but will compound with future callouts.

**Fix:**
```diff
- className="... ring-1 ring-[var(--color-blue)]/15 ..."
+ className="... border border-[var(--color-blue)]/20 ..."
```

**Suggested command:** Direct edit

## Persona Red Flags

### Elena (Senior Tech Recruiter — project-specific)
*Profile: 8+ years recruiting senior cloud engineers. Sees 200+ portfolios per month. Calibrated to detect AI-generated content. 10-second scan decides "call or pass." Arrives from LinkedIn on desktop.*

- **Font stack registers as AI default.** Inter Tight + Geist combo is now the Vercel portfolio fingerprint she's seen on 40% of "AI-polished" portfolios this year. She doesn't name it, but it registers as template.
- **Eyebrows erode the editorial voice.** The page positions itself as "briefing document, not sales page." Section eyebrows are the hallmark of a generated sales page. They undercut the tone.
- **"Stories" in nav may cause her to skip case studies.** If she's scanning for "work" or "projects," "Stories" doesn't map. She may reach Contact without having seen the strongest credential content.

### Jordan (First-Timer — first time visiting a cloud architect portfolio)
- **RecruiterScanner button in hero:** the label/context for this feature isn't immediately clear to a non-recruiter. What does clicking it do?
- **Experience cards open by default (first one).** This is good. Jordan sees content immediately without needing to click.
- **Chatbot is gated behind a launcher button.** Jordan likely won't find it unprompted. Fine for a portfolio, but worth noting.

### Casey (Distracted Mobile User)
- **Stats on mobile collapse to a flex-wrap row of tiny mono text** (`flex flex-wrap gap-x-5 gap-y-1`). This is the right call (no large grid on mobile), but the numbers are very small (`font-mono text-[11px]`). The hero stats become nearly invisible on small screens.
- **Primary CTA in hero** (`href="#contact"`) is below the fold on mobile. The Hiring Assistant launcher is positioned bottom-right (thumb-zone ✓). CTAs are mid-page but reachable.
- **Typewriter effect** plays fully on mobile even though the user's attention span on mobile is shorter. Scroll indicator appears once the typewriter finishes — but if the user scrolls before it's done, the indicator is missing.
