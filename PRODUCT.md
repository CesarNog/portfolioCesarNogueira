# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Senior tech recruiters, engineering directors, CTOs, and potential consulting clients — primarily European and international. Context: they find this link on LinkedIn, from a referral, or after a cold outreach. They scan for 10 seconds to decide if Cesar is worth a call. They are busy, skeptical of marketing fluff, and calibrated to detect AI-generated portfolios instantly.

Secondary: Cesar himself, using the site as a living CV and consulting pitch for UP2CLOUD engagements.

## Product Purpose

A premium personal portfolio and consulting pitch for Cesar Augusto Nogueira — Principal Cloud Architect, FinOps specialist, DevOps leader. The site must answer three questions in under 10 seconds: who is this, what has he built, and why should I contact him. Every section converts authority into contact intent. The site replaces a traditional CV; it is the artifact a recruiter sends up the chain.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Earned. International.

The tone is that of someone who has been in production at 3am and knows what it costs — not a thought-leader, not a consultant with a pitch deck, but an engineer with a track record. The site should feel like a briefing document, not a sales page. Confidence without bravado.

## Anti-references

- SaaS landing page templates (Webflow, Framer AI, v0-generated) — too bright, too much animation, too eager
- "AI portfolio syndrome": symmetric card grids, gradient badges, dashboard-widget credibility signals, fake terminal boot sequences
- Generic consultant sites with buzzword headlines: "Engineering the Future", "Precision for Tomorrow"
- Over-designed portfolios that put the tool before the person (the human should appear before the dashboard)

## Design Principles

1. **Person before product.** Face, name, and one human sentence before any metrics, widgets, or technical proof.
2. **Proof, not claims.** Specific numbers, named clients, concrete outcomes. No "world-class", "enterprise-grade", or "cutting-edge".
3. **Editorial restraint.** Every section gets one typographic move. Whitespace is the primary layout tool; borders are the last resort.
4. **Earned credibility.** Credentials, testimonials, and case studies feel documented, not marketed. The tone is post-mortem, not pitch deck.
5. **Globally legible.** Five languages (EN, PT, ES, FR, ZH). Accessible at WCAG AA minimum. Prints cleanly as a CV.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Verified contrast ratios on all text/background pairs. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected throughout — all Framer Motion animations have static fallbacks. Print stylesheet for CV output. `lang` attribute updated on language switch.
